I am finally back home and settled after the conference in Kananaskis. The whole experience was honestly so inspiring. Getting away from the lab for a few days to see what everyone else is working on really recharged my batteries.

The symposium was a blast from start to finish. I really enjoyed the different presentations, and I actually found myself developing a budding interest in advanced footwear technology. It is a cool intersection of Kinesiology and engineering that I hadn't given much thought to before. Aside from the academics, the food was great, and the midnight adventures with the group were definitely a highlight. We ended up watching baseball at one point, and even though I still have absolutely no understanding of how that sport works, the vibes were top tier.

One of the best parts was waking up early for a sunrise hike. Seeing the sun come up over the mountains was incredible and definitely made the early alarm worth it. Got some incredicly cool shots of the car too ^_^

The drive back was just as good as the drive up. The mountain scenery never gets old, and I even spotted a BMW M8 Competition on the road, which was a nice bonus for the drive. I also managed to find some of the darkest coffee I’ve ever tasted to keep me fueled for the trip home. It was the perfect way to wrap up a long weekend before getting back into the thick of my honors work.
