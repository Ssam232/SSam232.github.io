It was an absolutely freezing morning today, the kind where you just want to stay under the covers and ignore your alarms. But once I got into the Herzog Lab for our group presentation, something just clicked. I was scheduled to give a standard progress update, but I made a last-minute executive decision: I hijacked my own time slot to basically do a full dry run of my poster presentation instead.

It was a bit of a gamble, but I am so glad I did it because I was absolutely on fire today.

Thinking back to that rough update presentation I had a few months ago, this felt like night and day. The biggest difference was my pacing. I forced myself to be slower and significantly more composed. I did not just rush through the slides to get it over with; I actually let the data breathe and gave myself the space to explain the mechanics properly. By slowing down, I found that the natural enthusiasm I actually have for this project finally started bleeding through into my delivery.

The Q&A section was the real test, though. In the past, getting grilled on the methodology would spike my heart rate, but today I felt anchored. I was able to answer the questions transparently—if I didn't have a perfect answer, I didn't try to mask it, but I leaned into what the data _was_ telling us with a much higher degree of confidence. Not an arrogant "I know everything" kind of confidence, but a grounded trust in the work I have put in over the last year.

Walking out of the lab today, the cold didn't really bother me. It feels incredibly good to see tangible progress, not just in the data collection, but in how I am communicating it!
