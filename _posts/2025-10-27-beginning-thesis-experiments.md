After all the months of practicing fiber isolation and getting comfortable at the dissecting scope, I am beyond excited to say that the real thesis experiments have officially begun! 🎉

Here is the best part: I do not have to mount the fibers myself. That was honestly one of the things I was most nervous about going in. Mounting a single muscle fiber onto the testing apparatus without damaging it is incredibly delicate work, and after watching Venus do it so effortlessly while I struggled through practice sessions, I was not exactly brimming with confidence. But my role in the experiment flow means I get to skip that step entirely and focus on the actual data collection and analysis side of things. I am not going to lie, that is a huge relief.

Now for the part that has been keeping my brain busy: the math. The mechanical testing generates raw force and length data, but turning that into meaningful stress-strain relationships and deriving the actual material properties of the myofilaments involves some pretty intense calculations. We are talking about converting raw force outputs into stress by accounting for cross-sectional area, calculating sarcomere length changes, and then fitting the data to exponential and polynomial models to extract passive and active force-length characteristics. There are a lot of moving pieces.

Rather than crunching all of that by hand or writing scripts from scratch, I built out a comprehensive Excel sheet with all the formulae baked in. You plug in your raw data and it handles the unit conversions, the cross-sectional area normalization, the stress-strain calculations, and even generates the key plots automatically. It took a solid chunk of time to set up and validate, but now that it is running, it saves an enormous amount of time per experiment. One sheet, all the math, no room for manual calculation errors. It is honestly one of the things I am most proud of so far in this project.

The first few experimental runs have gone smoothly and I am starting to see real data come together. There is nothing quite like watching months of preparation finally translate into actual results on a screen. This is what I have been working toward all year and it feels amazing to finally be here. Time to generate some serious data. 🔬📊
