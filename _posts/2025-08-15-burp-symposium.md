I participated in the BURP Symposium, where I delivered a poster presentation titled "Structural Properties of the Female Heart Following a High Fat and Sucrose Diet." This experience served as a pivotal precursor to my honors thesis, allowing me to explore the intricate Structural shifts in cardiac tissue induced by metabolic stress. Presenting this research to a diverse academic audience sharpened my ability to communicate complex myofilament mechanics and experimental data effectively. The quality of the work and the clarity of my delivery were recognized with a 3rd place award in the poster competition, a milestone that solidified my passion for cardiovascular research and provided a strong foundation for the more advanced honors project that followed in my final year.
