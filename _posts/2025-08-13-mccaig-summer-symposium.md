On July 13, 2025, I had the opportunity to present our research at the McCaig Summer Symposium here in Calgary. My poster detailed our investigation into the structural properties of cardiac muscle in female rats following a 12-week high-fat and high-sucrose diet. Working out of the Human Performance Lab with Dr. Herzog and the rest of the team , we had initially hypothesized that the diet would compromise the structural integrity of the heart. However, our tissue analysis revealed that the female rat hearts remained structurally intact, showing no significant differences in average cardiac protein or collagen concentrations compared to the control group. Sharing these findings with the McCaig Institute community and discussing how estrogen-mediated cardio-protection likely acts as a buffer against early diet-induced remodeling was a crucial milestone that perfectly set the stage for the deeper myofilament mechanics work I'm tackling in my honors thesis today
