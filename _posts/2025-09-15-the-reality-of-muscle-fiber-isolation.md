Okay, the honeymoon phase of the thesis is officially over. I am finally starting to realize the actual scale of the work I signed up for.

I always knew muscle fibers were tiny since we see them in textbook diagrams all the time. But knowing that in theory and actually trying to isolate one under a microscope are very different things. Right now I am practicing with rabbit psoas tissue. Most of my day is just me hunched over a dissecting scope holding a pair of ridiculously sharp forceps.

I am quickly learning that my tweezer skills need a lot of work. When you are zoomed in that close, even just breathing too heavily makes your hands shake enough to look like an earthquake through the lens. You have to be incredibly careful to tease apart the tissue bundles fiber by fiber. If you slip up or pull too hard, you damage the myofilaments and ruin the sample.

The crazy part is watching my supervisor Venus do it. She can sit down at the scope and perfectly isolate and mount a fiber in about five minutes flat. It is honestly wild to watch and it really shows just how much experience she has. I will sit there for what feels like hours struggling while she just makes it look completely effortless.

It is definitely tedious and my eyes are feeling the strain. It takes a ton of patience, but when you finally manage to isolate a clean, intact fiber and get it mounted properly, it feels great. The actual mechanical testing is going to be a serious grind. At least my hands are getting steadier though.
