Today was the official dry run for my upcoming presentation at the Alberta Biomedical Engineering symposium. I am fully prepared to make my grand return to BME after a two year hiatus, so everyone better be ready.

I am presenting all the data I put together over the summer regarding the structural properties of female rat hearts following a high fat and high sucrose diet. To be completely honest, the poster itself is still looking a little shaky right now. Trying to condense months of tissue analysis and collagen concentration data into one cohesive, easy to follow story is proving to be harder than I thought.

The dry run with the lab definitely highlighted a few of those weak spots. I got a lot of feedback on my flow and how I am explaining the estrogen cardio-protection angle. It is always a little humbling to get critiqued, but I am taking it in stride. Their feedback was incredibly constructive and exactly what I needed to hear to actually make this presentation good.

There is definitely still progress being made despite the rough edges today. I have a very clear list of edits to tackle and enough time to iron out the remaining kinks before the actual symposium. It is a work in progress, but I know it is going to be solid by the time I am actually standing next to the board.
